Retrieval-Augmented Dialogue Knowledge Aggregation for Expressive Conversational Speech Synthesis
Abstract
Conversational speech synthesis (CSS) aims to take the current dialogue history (CDH) as reference to synthesize expressive speech that aligns with the conversational style. Unlike CDH, historical dialogue history (HDH) contains dialogue logs preserved from earlier periods of user-agent interaction, which also contains rich conversational style expression knowledge. Note that this knowledge also play an important role in how the agent can better synthesize expressive conversational speech to generate empathetic feedback. However, previous research overlooks this aspect. To address this issue, we propose a novel Retrieval-Augmented Knowledge Aggregation scheme for expressive CSS, termed ReKA-CSS, that includes three main components: 1) To effectively retrieve style knowledge from HDH, we employ a two-stage retrieval-augmented mechanism. In stage 1, we match CDH to HDH dialogues for style similarity and retrieve the most similar Top K dialogues. In stage 2, we propose a multi-granularity heterogeneous graph (MgHG) to model retrieved dialogue. Subsequently, the dialogue style-linguistic contrastive learning module extracts dialogue-level style-related text features from the dialogue text. 2) For CDH, we also employ the MgHG structure to capture the rich style knowledge. 3) At last, these two kinds of style knowledge of CDH and HDH are aggregated and fed to the speech synthesizer to help the CSS model synthesize expressive speech that conforms to the conversational style. Both objective and subjective evaluations suggest that our model outperforms the baseline models in expressiveness rendering. Code and audio samples can be found at: https://github.com/AAAI2025-CSS/ReKA-CSS.

Experiments
Comparative Experiment:
1) DailyTalk is an advanced CTTS baseline. It proposes coarse-grained text context modeling in dialogue history to enhance speech expressiveness.
2) M²-CTTS designs a textual context module and an acoustic context module with both coarse-grained and fine-grained modeling, generating speech with better prosody and naturalness.
3) Homogeneous Graph-based CSS adopts the homogeneous graph to model the multi-modal context in conversation, inferring both the global and local speaking styles of the current utterance.
4) CONCSS incorporates a contrastive learning module to ensure the discriminability of contextual features during dialogue history modeling.
5) ECSS is an emotional expressive conversational speech synthesis model. It constructs multi-source knowledge into a heterogeneous graph and incorporates an emotion prediction task to synthesize emotionally expressive speech.

Case Study

We present the current dialogue along with the Top 1, Top 2, Top 3, and Top 10 dialogues retrieved by ReKA-CSS that are most similar in conversational style to the current dialogue, as well as the Bottom 1, Bottom 2, Bottom 3, and Bottom 10 dialogues that are the least similar in conversational style.

The similarity between conversational styles is calculated using cosine similarity. We found that the scenes of dialogues with similar styles are remarkably similar to the current dialogue scene, while the scenes of dialogues with dissimilar styles are completely unrelated to the current dialogue scene. Additionally, in terms of subjective perception, it is easy to sense that the styles of the retrieved similar dialogues closely match the current dialogue, while dialogues with dissimilar styles are also distinctly different in terms of auditory perception.
